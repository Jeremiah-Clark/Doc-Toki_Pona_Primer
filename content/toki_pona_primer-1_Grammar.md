# Grammar

## Basic Sentence Structure

```
X li [B] Y [e Z] [C W]
```

| Part | Role                         |
|------|------------------------------|
| `X`  | Subject                      |
| `li` | Predicate marker             |
| `B`  | Preverb (optional)           |
| `Y`  | Predicate                    |
| `e`  | Direct-object marker         |
| `Z`  | Direct object (optional)     |
| `C`  | Preposition (optional)       |
| `W`  | Additional phrase (optional) |

## Pronouns

Used for both singular and plural.

| Word   | Meaning           | Notes     |
| ------ | ----------------- | --------- |
| `mi`   | I, me, we         | Omit `li` |
| `sina` | you               | Omit `li` |
| `ona`  | he, she, it, they | Use `li`  |
| `ni`   | this, that        | Use `li`  |

> [!TIP]
> 
> `mi` and `sina` **never** take `li` ‚Äî the predicate follows directly.
> 
> `mi kama. mi lukin. mi pakala` ‚Äî *"I came, I saw, I conquered."*

## Predicates

The predicate is what the subject **is** or **does**. 
It can be:

- An **action**‚Äîworking, playing, talking
- A **description**‚Äîblue, good, tall
- A **thing**‚Äîa house, an animal, food

## Preverbs

Placed *before* the predicate: 
`X li <preverb> Y`

| Word    | Meaning                            |
|------|------------------------------|
| `awen`  | continuous state                   |
| `kama`  | became / is becoming / will become |
| `ken`   | to be able to                      |
| `lukin` | to try to see or notice            |
| `sona`  | to know how to                     |
| `wile`  | to want / to need                  |

> [!TIP]
> 
> `mi sona` = *"I know"* vs. `mi kama sona` = *"I'm learning"* (moving toward knowledge)

## Direct Object

Marked by `e`. 
The thing the action is done **to**.

- `soweli li pali e tomo waso` ‚Äî *"The animal builds a nest."*
- `mi lukin e mun` ‚Äî *"I watch the stars."*
- `mi moku kili` = *I eat fruit (general)* vs. `mi moku e kili` = *I'm eating fruit (specific)*

## Prepositions

Further modify predicates with location, manner, reason, etc.

| Word      | Meaning                           |
|------|------------------------------|
| `kepeken` | making use of, by way of          |
| `lon`     | location in relation to something |
| `sama`    | similar/equal to                  |
| `tan`     | the origin of, because of         |
| `tawa`    | towards, from the perspective of  |

> [!TIP]
>
> Use `lon` before a relational word to identify a spatial relationship.
>
> `soweli li lon insa tomo` ‚Äî *"The animal is in the house."*

## Particles

Small words with grammatical functions.

| Word  | Function                                    |
|------|------------------------------|
| `anu` | either/or (combines subjects or predicates) |
| `e`   | marks the direct object                     |
| `en`  | and (combines multiple subjects)            |
| `la`  | context marker                              |
| `li`  | marks the predicate                         |
| `o`   | command or vocative                         |
| `pi`  | marks a modifier phrase                     |

### `li` ‚Äî Predicate Marker

Omitted only when the subject is `mi` or `sina`.

- `soweli li suwi` ‚Äî *"The animal is cute."*
- `ona li pali mute li lape lili` ‚Äî *"They work a lot and sleep a bit."* (multiple predicates: repeat `li`)

### `e` ‚Äî Direct Object Marker

`X li Y e Z` = X does Y to Z

- `soweli li pakala e kasi` ‚Äî *"The animal breaks the plant."*

### `pi` ‚Äî Modifier Phrase

Groups multiple words into a single modifier for the head noun.

- `poki pi telo wawa` ‚Äî *"strong-liquid container"* (e.g. a coffee cup)
- `poki telo wawa` ‚Äî *"strong liquid container"* (e.g. a diamond bottle)

### `o` ‚Äî Command / Vocative

Replaces `li` for commands, wishes, or desires. 
The subject `sina` can be omitted in direct commands.

- `[sina] o tawa pona` ‚Äî *"Drive carefully!"*
- `ona o lape` ‚Äî *"They should sleep."*
- `jan Su o lape pona` ‚Äî *"Hey Su, sleep well!"*

### `la` ‚Äî Context Marker

`X la Y` = *In the context of X, Y*

- `tenpo lon la mi sitelen e lipu sona` ‚Äî *"Right now, I write a knowledge-document."*
- `mi la ni li pona` ‚Äî *"As for me, this is nice."*

### `en` ‚Äî Subject Combiner

Always omitted for the **first** subject.

- `jan en soweli li sama mute` ‚Äî *"People and animals are very similar."*

### `anu` ‚Äî Either/Or

- `waso anu kala li tawa` ‚Äî *"The bird or the fish moves."*
- Use to ask either/or questions: `telo suli anu telo lili ni` = *"Is this the ocean or a lake?"*

## Pseudo-Particles

Words that mostly act as particles but can function in other ways too.

| Word    | Meaning               |
|------|------------------------------|
| `ala`   | negation, nothingness |
| `kin`   | also, too             |
| `nanpa` | number, ordinal       |
| `taso`  | only, but             |

### `ala` ‚Äî Negation

Append to negate a phrase.

- `mi pali ala` ‚Äî *"I will not work."*
- `mi ken ala toki` ‚Äî *"I cannot speak."*

### `nanpa` ‚Äî Ordinal / Number

- `ilo pi akesi suwi nanpa wan` ‚Äî *"the first lizard's tool"*
- `ilo nanpa wan pi akesi suwi` ‚Äî *"the lizard's first tool"*

### `kin` ‚Äî Also / Too

- `jan li ken pona kin` ‚Äî *"People can also be peaceful."*
- `ona kin li tawa sona` ‚Äî *"They too gain knowledge."*

### `taso` ‚Äî Only / But

- `soweli li ken pona taso` ‚Äî *"Animals can only be good."*
- `taso ma li sike e suno` ‚Äî *"But, the earth revolves around the sun."*
- `ona li taso` ‚Äî *"They are alone."*

## How to Say "And"

There is no direct word for "and." 
Use these constructions:

| Situation                        | Construction       | Example            |
| -------------------------------- | ------------------ | ------------------ |
| Multiple subjects                | `en`               | `X1 en X2 li Y`    |
| Multiple predicates              | repeat `li`        | `X li Y1 li Y2`    |
| Multiple predicates with mi/sina | separate sentences | `mi Y1. mi Y2.`    |
| Multiple direct objects          | repeat `e`         | `X li Y e Z1 e Z2` |

## Questions

| Method    | Construction      | Meaning              |
| --------- | ----------------- | -------------------- |
| Yes/no    | `X li Y ala Y`    | *"Is X Y?"*          |
| Either/or | `X li Y anu seme` | *"Is X Y, or what?"* |
| Open      | `X li seme`       | *"What is/does X?"*  |
| Object    | `X li Y e seme`   | *"What does X Y?"*   |

## Negation

Append `ala` to the predicate:

- `mi tawa ala` ‚Äî *"I will not go."*
- `mi lon ala tomo` ‚Äî *"I am not in the house."*

## Comparison

Toki Pona offers several ways to compare one thing to another:

1. Put two short comparative sentences one after another.
- `jan Emily li suli. jan Trisha li lili` ‚Äî *"Emily is bigger than Trisha."*
2. Use `la` to present one item in the context of another:
- `tomo mi la tomo sina li loje mute` ‚Äî *"Compared to my house, your house is more red."*
3. Use `tawa`, in the sense of ‚Äúto,‚Äù to express perspective.
- `kala ni li lili tawa waso ni` ‚Äî *"That fish is small to that bird."*
4. Use `lon poka` (next to) for physical comparison:
- `kili ni li suli lon poka lawa mi` ‚Äî *‚ÄúThis fruit is big next to my head.‚Äù*
5. For superlatives, declare something `nanpa wan` (number one):
- `toki ni li pona nanpa wan a` ‚Äî *"This is the best language."*

## Transitive Statements

`X li Y e Z` can mean:

1. **X applies Y to Z** ‚Äî `mi pona e tomo` Û±¶∞ *"I repair the house."*
2. **X causes Z to become Y** ‚Äî `mi tomo e waso` Û±¶∞ *"I turn the bird into a house."*

## Interjections & Emoticle

`a` is the *emoticle* (emotion + particle) ‚Äî append to any word or phrase to indicate excitement or emphasis.

- `pona a` ‚Äî *"So nice!"*
- `wawa tawa sina a` ‚Äî *"May the force be with you!"*
